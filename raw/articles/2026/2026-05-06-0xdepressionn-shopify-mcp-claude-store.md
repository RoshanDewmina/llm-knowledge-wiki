Someone spent last weekend setting up a Shopify store.

Not "use AI to write a description." Actually connect. Your store, your products, your orders, all of it, running inside the chat.

The setup most people missed

Shopify launched an MCP server.

MCP (Model Context Protocol) is a protocol that lets AI assistants connect directly to external tools and databases. When Shopify turned it on, Claude stopped being a writing tool for e-commerce and became an operator.

You type. The store updates.

"Add 40 products to my summer collection" and Claude does it. "Show me which products had zero orders this month" and Claude pulls the report. "Rewrite all descriptions in a tone that converts better" and Claude rewrites them.

The whole operation: products, orders, collections, customers, discounts. All accessible.

Shopify also open-sourced their AI Toolkit on April 9th, 2026. It's on GitHub under MIT license. Meaning anyone can build on top of this.

The barrier didn't just get lower. It got removed.

1 / 3 | PRODUCT SELECTION: $500-2,000/month saved on research

Most people spend weeks on this part.

They pay for Jungle Scout, Helium 10, or a research agency. They look at trends, check margins, analyze competition, calculate shipping costs.

Claude does all of it in one conversation.

The key is giving it the right inputs. Tell it your budget, your target market, what you want to avoid, what's already working in your niche.

Сlaude do the same as JungleScout

→ Product Research Prompt:

plaintext
You are an e-commerce product researcher. I want to find 5 winning product ideas for a Shopify store.

My budget for inventory: [your budget]
Target audience: [describe your audience]
Niche I'm interested in: [your niche]
Products I want to avoid: [list what you don't want]

For each product idea give me:
1. Product name and description
2. Estimated supplier cost (AliExpress or local)
3. Suggested retail price and margin %
4. Why it's likely to sell right now (trends, seasons, demand signals)
5. Main competitors selling this and their price
6. One specific reason this product could fail and how to avoid it

Be specific. No generic advice. I need products I can actually find and sell this week.

→ Competitor Analysis Prompt:

plaintext
I'm selling [product] on Shopify. My main competitors are [list 2-3 stores or brands].

Analyze what they're doing:
1. What price points are they using?
2. What do their top reviews say customers love most?
3. What do negative reviews say customers hate?
4. Where is the obvious gap I can fill?

Give me 3 angles I can use to position my store differently and win on [price / quality / niche / service].

basic research (Google + gut feeling) = 2 weeks + wrong product 60% of the time research agency = $500-2,000/month Claude doing it properly = 2 hours + $0

metric to watch: margin after shipping and returns. If it's under 30%, the product is fragile to any ad cost increase.

This is where the Shopify MCP changes everything.

Before, you needed a developer to configure your store. A copywriter to write 50 product descriptions. A designer to organize your collections.

Now you need a Shopify subscription and Claude.

Connect your store to Claude via the Shopify MCP. Then start giving orders.

admin products page

→ Product Descriptions Prompt (run inside Claude with MCP connected):

I need product descriptions for my Shopify store. My brand voice is [describe: casual / premium / technical / friendly].

My target customer: [describe who buys from you]

For each product description:
- First line: one specific benefit, not a feature
- Second paragraph: what problem it solves and how
- Third paragraph: what makes it different from what they've seen before
- End with one line that makes them feel like they'd be making a mistake not to buy

Write descriptions for: [list your products]

Keep each description under 150 words. No filler. No "high quality." No "you'll love it."

→ Store Structure Prompt:

plaintext
I'm setting up a Shopify store selling [product category].

Help me build the collection structure:
1. What collections should I create?
2. How should I organize products inside each collection?
3. What should the homepage feature first?
4. Write the homepage hero text (headline + subheadline + CTA button text)

My brand: [describe it]
My main customer's biggest problem: [describe it]
Price range of my products: [range]

→ Pricing Strategy Prompt:

I sell [product] at [current price]. My cost per unit is [cost].

Tell me:
1. Is my pricing positioned correctly for [my target market]?
2. Should I test higher or lower? What's the ceiling?
3. What bundle or upsell would increase average order value?
4. Write me 3 pricing page variants (cheapest / middle / premium) with names and what to include in each tier.

hiring a Shopify developer = $3,000-5,000 one-time setup + $500/month maintenance copywriter for 50 product descriptions = $1,500-3,000 one-time Claude with MCP doing all of it = $0 outside of Claude subscription

mistake to avoid: don't give Claude your full product catalog in one prompt. Do it in batches of 10-15. The quality drops when it's handling 100 products at once.

→ Ad Copy Prompt:

I'm running Facebook/Instagram ads for my Shopify store selling [product].

My target audience: [age, interests, problem they have]
My product's main benefit (not feature): [one line]
My price: [price] / competitor average: [competitor price]

Write me 5 ad variations:
1. Pain-first (open with the problem, offer the solution)
2. Proof-first (open with a result or stat, explain how)
3. Curiosity-first (open with a question or surprising fact)
4. Direct offer (price, what they get, deadline if any)
5. Story-first (30-second story format, personal tone)

Each ad: headline + primary text + CTA. Keep primary text under 125 words.

→ Creative Brief Prompt (for ChatGPT or designer):

Create a visual brief for a Facebook ad creative.

Product: [product]
Ad angle: [which of the 5 you're running]
Target audience: [describe]

Describe:
1. What should be in the image or video (scene, people, environment)
2. What text overlay should say (headline only, max 5 words)
3. Color palette that matches [your brand colors]
4. One specific emotion this creative should make the viewer feel
5. Reference: describe a real ad you've seen that has similar energy

Keep the brief under 200 words. Make it concrete enough that a designer can work without asking questions.

→ Retargeting Strategy Prompt:

I'm running a Shopify store selling [product] at [price].

Help me build a retargeting strategy for people who visited but didn't buy:

1. What's the most likely reason they didn't buy?
2. What should my retargeting ad say to address that reason?
3. Should I offer a discount? How much? What's the risk?
4. What's the ideal retargeting window (1 day / 3 days / 7 days) for this product type?

Write me 2 retargeting ad scripts addressing the top 2 objections.
