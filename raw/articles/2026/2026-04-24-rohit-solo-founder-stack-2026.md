The article frames the solo-founder stack as a team chart rather than a tools list:

- Engineer: Claude Code, onboarded with `CLAUDE.md`, repeatable skills, and MCP integrations.
- Back office: five pipelines replacing content editor, SDR, analyst, bookkeeper, and support hire.
- Research/marketing: Higgsfield Marketing Studio plus Hermes/OpenClaw for upstream research.
- Distribution/persona layer: AI persona/face/voice for repeatable video ads.

Claude Code setup:

1. Write `CLAUDE.md` at repo root with codebase conventions, DB/auth details, tests, and boundaries.
2. Build skills as markdown procedures for recurring task classes: ship-feature, triage-bug, migrate-schema.
3. Plug in MCP integrations for GitHub, Postgres, Slack, monitoring, Linear, and filesystem/tool access.

Back-office pipelines named:

- YouTube/video repurposing into platform-ready posts.
- Lead enrichment + first-touch email.
- Competitive intelligence over competitor sites.
- Invoice/document extraction into Xero.
- Knowledge-base support reply drafting with citations.

Marketing research layer:

- Distinguishes Higgsfield's internal “Hermes Agent” from Nous Research's open-source Hermes Agent.
- Suggests Hermes for scheduled cloud/public-web research and OpenClaw for local file/inbox/vault research.
- Names three first research jobs: scrape top 50 Meta ads weekly and cluster hooks; monitor X/Reddit for high-reach niche complaints; mine support tickets for customer pain phrases.
