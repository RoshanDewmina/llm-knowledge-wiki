# The solo founder stack of 2026

## Topic Map

- Claude Code as onboarded engineer.
- `CLAUDE.md`, skills, MCP as workspace setup.
- Back-office automation pipelines.
- Marketing research agents and Higgsfield.
- Hermes vs OpenClaw positioning.

## Useful Notes

- Best concrete takeaway: treat agent setup like onboarding a senior hire. The codebase needs conventions, skills, and tool access.
- Marketing/distribution is framed as the bottleneck after coding becomes cheap; research agents should feed exact customer language and competitor gaps into creative production.
- The article explicitly warns about a naming collision: Higgsfield's internal “Hermes Agent” is not Nous Research's open-source Hermes Agent.
- Suggested research jobs are practical: weekly Meta ads hook clustering, high-reach X/Reddit complaint monitoring, and support-ticket language mining.

## Verified Claims

- The article says Claude Code needs `CLAUDE.md`, skills, and MCP to act like an engineer rather than autocomplete.
- The article names five back-office pipeline categories.
- The article positions Hermes for scheduled public-web research and OpenClaw for local-file/inbox research.
