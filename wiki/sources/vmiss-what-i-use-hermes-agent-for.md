# What I Use Hermes Agent For (And How I Use It)

## Topic Map

- Problem-first agent design.
- Multi-profile Hermes setup.
- Research/execution split.
- Lifestyle and health-support agents.
- Cost-minimized model/provider routing.
- Local-model practicality on modest hardware.

## Useful Notes

- Start by logging your day/week, then automate or delegate repeated low-value tasks. The source explicitly rejects starting from hardware/model hype.
- Treat AI as assistant, not replacement: make it do grunt work, verify, and only automate things you already understand.
- Separate profiles by job: research agent for citations/learning, task-master/executor for building, lifestyle agent for reminders, lifestyle/research agent for personal constraints.
- Cheap personal agent stack can mix OpenRouter free models, Nous Portal subscription, local llama.cpp models, ChatGPT Plus/Codex, NVIDIA NIM, and possibly DeepSeek.
- Local models are positioned as surprisingly useful for narrow lifestyle/research tasks even on 8GB VRAM or M1/16GB.

## Verified Claims

- The author runs multiple Hermes profiles/agents through TUI and Telegram.
- The author uses a research agent for cited briefs and learning, not blind execution.
- The author uses reminders and chronic-health/food research as “soft” life workflows.
- The author emphasizes cost control and model/provider experimentation.

## Evidence Extracts

### ex-assistant-not-replacement

> My personal philosophy when it comes to AI is I treat AI as my assistant.  I don’t use it to replace my thinking, I use it to point me in the right direction.  I make it do grunt work, then I verify and proceed.

### ex-start-from-friction

> What I have found to be extremely helpful was writing down things I did for a day, then taking a good look at that list... “What are the things that took a lot of time?” and “What are things that I have to do, but didn’t provide a lot of value to my workflow?”.

### ex-agent-crew

> Here is a break down of my current agent crew, and what provider/model they use currently.  I access them using the Hermes TUI and over Telegram.

### ex-cost-warning

> I am on a personal mission to do this as cheap as possible... horror stories of people just connecting to the Anthropic API and spending hundreds of dollars a day.  No thank you.
